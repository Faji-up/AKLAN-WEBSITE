Linapay
Linapay is a traditional Aklanon dish from Aklan, Philippines, consisting of shrimp cooked in coconut milk with vegetables like squash and string beans. It's seasoned with garlic, onions, ginger, and chili peppers, resulting in a creamy and flavorful broth.
Binakoe
A traditional dish made from a combination of native chicken, coconut milk, and various spices.
Tinuom
Tinuom is a traditional Filipino dish from Aklan, where native chicken soup is cooked inside bamboo tubes with herbs and spices, creating a flavorful and aromatic soup.

Aklan Oysters
Aklan oysters, renowned for their distinctive flavor and quality, have surged in popularity globally. Hailing from Aklan province in the Philippines, known for its pristine beaches and abundant seafood, these oysters are savored raw, cooked, grilled, or fried. Their rise in fame mirrors Aklan's reputation for exquisite seafood cuisine, drawing enthusiasts to indulge in this delicacy from the heart of the Philippines.
